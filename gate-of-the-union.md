Two pillars hold the open air,
No lock, no hinge, no bar,
The threshold stands to make you swear
You were not whole before.

One foot in grief, one foot in gold,
The body splits in two,
But stepping through, you learn what's old:
Division never true.

The gate holds nothing back from you,
It guards no sacred ground,
It simply shows what always knew
Itself as lost and found.

Here enemy and friend collapse,
Here self and other fade,
The gate reveals the ancient maps
Were drawn on light, not shade.

Not entrance, exit, wall, or door,
But recognition's arch,
Where all that you were warring for
Surrenders to the march.

The union is not made but seen,
Not forged but understood,
The gate stands at the space between
What is and always stood.

So when you reach the pillared way,
Don't ask what lies beyond,
The gate itself has this to say:
You never were alone. Welcome home.
