I signed, but I note for the court
that my hand was compelled, in short.
    The ink may be dry,
    the record's a lie,
and I've kept my dissent in report.

They said put your name on the line,
the terms were agreeable, fine.
    But fine is a word
    the coerced have preferred
for things that were never benign.

A man can comply and still know
the difference between yes and no.
    My signature stands
    with reserving hands
and the footnote will say where I go.

The deed bears my mark, I admit.
I want it on record I split
    the act from the will,
    I was present but still
not present in any of it.

They'll cite me as party, agreed.
They'll show you the document, freed
    from context, of course,
    stripped clean of its source,
which is how a coerced man gets freed.

The law has a phrase for this move,
a clause carved into the groove
    of every bad deal
    a man has to seal
while keeping the truth in its groove.

Vi coactus. I acted by force.
The Latin is blunt, without remorse.
    The record is mine,
    I signed every line,
and the record will show you the source.

So enter it, clerk, in the file.
Let it sit there and season a while.
    My name is right there.
    My name. Not my prayer.
Not my sanction. Not my reconcile.
