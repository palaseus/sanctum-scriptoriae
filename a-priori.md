The child who's never touched the flame
already pulls her hand away
some ancient circuit speaks her name
in languages before the day.

I knew the depth before I drowned,
the way a sparrow knows the air,
how roots can feel their way through ground
toward water they've not tasted there.

The heart keeps time to rhythms old
as tides that move the sleeping sea,
a mathematics we were told
before we learned to count or see.

Your face was familiar when we met,
as if my blood had drawn the map
the body's long-remembered debt
to forms that fit like hand in lap.

Some knowledge lives inside the bone,
the way a seed contains the tree,
a theorem we have always known
but never proved empirically.

The mind unfolds what it contains:
these truths that need no teacher's hand,
like how the newborn infant strains
toward breast, toward warmth, toward motherland.
