The first shot found his quiet door,
A breakfast table torn by flame;
War announced itself once more
As if it learned to speak his name.

He fled the sound, he sought a place
Where guns were meant to never come,
Yet fate has little use for grace
When history demands a sum.

Four years weighed heavy on the land,
Till uniforms filled parlor air;
The end was signed by steady hand
Where peace had once been meant to bear.
