""Walter Russell"

What of a man? To draw the light before he found its name,
A cosmic blueprint burning eternally in his frame,
While others measured atoms, playing it safe and small,
The universe fell out of his lead; from the rise, to the fall.

From marble blocks he carved what imagination could see,
The very form of thought and the same of energy.
His chisel spoke in frequencies ringing divine,
Converting space to art, and chaos to ordered line.

They called him mad! Him! The prophet of the wave,
Who said that death was just what matter gave
To consciousness that never learned to die,
Knighting us spiral dancers in an endless sky.

He saw no shine in the sun, for it's violence radiates,
That it is the stillness that holds the power that creates,
For every atom is little more than a thought compressed,
And love's the force that puts the void to rest.

At ninety-three sharper, still, than polished glass,
He told the scholars all their truths would truly pass.
That light and dark is, at no point, separate at all,
But lovers gracefully stepping at creation's ball.

Painting instrumental symphonies, writing music's laws,
Raised monuments to being, without refrain or pause,
A Renaissance soul delivered into an electric age,
Left the remnants of his cosmic origins dripping from canvas page.

They sleep on genius until the genius is gone,
But truth outlives the tomb it's written on,
He knew that all we seek can be found only in all we are,
It is the same void within us, is the same between each star.

Save your light and holy water, warrantless to the prophet in the dark
For they see the fire some miles, or years, before they see the spark,
Who live like lightning strikes anchored unto human skin,
The architects of where we are and where we've never been.

He left us maps to territories as newfound as they are vast:
The present's just a pleasant interruption to the past
And for every future ending is yet another door
These waves of our will touch infinity, without descent, as we lament upon the shore.
