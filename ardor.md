The only time you should vacate your throne is while you're standing up for something you believe in
the rest is ceremonial; cold marble, and the long deceiving.
A crown is just the weight of what you haven't done yet,
a promise to yourself the mirror helps you to forget.

I've watched men grip their armrests through a flood,
calling the water holy when the drought is in their blood.
But sovereignty is motion in the very marrow of the bone,
you cannot rule the living from a perfectly kept throne.

The learned, gilded oak drops its acorns into mud,
surrenders its own empire to the seasons and the flood,
and still commands the hillside as both the tallest and the first
because it gave itself to rain before it ever gave to thirst.

So rise. And in the rising feel that old wood lose its pull.
The seat will hold your shape awhile, may the room remain half-full
of whoever you were sitting there-
that careful, measured, titled man who mistook stillness for despair.

What you believe in has an address. It is standing out in the cold.
You won't find it in the charter for it is neither written nor is it sold.
It's the thing that makes your heart move before your mind says go,
and the altar you keep returning to regardless of the snow.

Vacate your throne for that and when you sit again,
you won't be sitting. You'll be reigning.
