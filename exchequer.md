He did not need the crown to be the king,
he only needed everything to bear his name.
The tower rose in gold above the common street,
not beauty, but a signature in concrete and in sleet.

In concrete and in sleet, the debt was always planned,
six bankruptcies, six lessons in the ledger's sleight of hand.
The creditor who stretched the line became the one in need,
for debt beyond a certain size inverts the one who bleeds.

Beyond the one who bleeds, the shadow falls before the man,
the man is just the instrument that makes the shadow's span
believable to those who need a thing too large to fail,
a name too pressed in deep to pull, a building not for sale.

Not for sale, the table where the unseen columns meet,
the room that holds no minutes, where no record finds its seat.
The cloth is checked, the ledger closed, the building still remains,
the exchequer needs no throne to hold the ones who hold the reins.
