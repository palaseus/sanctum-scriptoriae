Joy without intention is motion unconfined,
Before the first phoneme could name or find,
There danced a being in the space between
The word for thing and what the thing might mean.

B is for Bombadil, but he came first,
Before the letter learned to slake its thirst
For meaning, mapping, making order known,
He was the chuckle that bounced off echoed stone.

The scholars say that naming carries the power,
That Adam's true gift was language as a tower
From which to see and sort and understand,
But he was there singing back when soil was sand.

He knows the secret that the vowels keep,
The reason consonants refuse to sleep:
That every symbol is a container, a kind of cage,
And freedom lives long before the written page.

B is for Beginning, but he's older, still,
B is for the Boundary, but bolder he feels
Than any line we draw to keep things clear,
He predates parallels and glass, he has no peer.

The corruption of power found in him no door,
For doors require a frame, a wall, a floor,
And Bombadil is what the frame forgot,
The is that simply is and answers it's not.

So when you learn your letters, bittersweet and young,
Remember some things danced before they sung,
That B might be for Being, bare and bright,
The good thing itself before we called it light.

The ancient intelligence is this:
That knowing is a kind of violence you miss
Turned into contract, wonder turned to sheer fact,
The bomb or the deal signals the final act.

There will he be, still, when the last book turns to dust,
When alphabets collapse back into rust,
Still singing in the syntax reduced to whisper, then forgot,
The grammar of the is, the was, and the not.
