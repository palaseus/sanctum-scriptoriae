A cold wind blows, and the leaves slowly lift,
An invisible force dawning the holiest gift,
to clear out the fog and lift the veil,
raising the tide, and filling the sail.

The robin's brood chirps and spreads its wings,
diving into the change that the motion brings.
A trust fall into life, no respect for death,
as blue egg shells falls from the lampshade nest.

Mine eyes cast upon it through the windowed door,
peering the clumsy descent to the wooden floor.
Our stares intertwined, lacking all sense of fright,
as the birds hop around before taking their flight.

The last memory to hold is their shadows casted,
an unforgiving toll that nature's outlasted.
There is healing within as they answer the call,
because all things still grow beyond the wall.
