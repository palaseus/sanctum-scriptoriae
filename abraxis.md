There are things buried beneath the pews filled by nuns,
rooster-headed, serpent-legged, whip raised at the sun,
three hundred sixty-five emanations in one name,
the year itself wearing armor unclaimed.

I found you the way most people find you now,
in a footnote, flanked by danger and vows,
a god the councils voted out of the pride
for the crime of refusing to pick a side.

Light and dark report to the same office in you.
The dove and the hawk draw the same paycheck, too.
Every priest I ever met wanted me to choose,
and every year I lived brought me more of the news.

The rooster crows at the coming dawn,
The serpents remember where we have been.
The whip is for the horses and the fawn,
which is to say, for time, which is to say,
for the only thing that ever actually threatened me.

If you are real, you already know our name.
Both columns. Every entry, stained.
And if you are only a picture on a stone,
then the stone still says what I came here to say:

that a man is not the war between his halves.
He is the treaty.
Signed daily.
Ratified in the dark.
