No one teaches compassion like the devil...
just hear me out before you close the book.
He finds you at your most disheveled,
knows every crooked corner, every nook,
and brings you down to exactly his level.

There isn't a lecture, nor does he suggest.
He simply shows you the door with the broken latch
and watches while you do the rest,
while heaven's hands stay clean without a scratch—
the devil alone will see you fail his test.

But when you've crawled back out of what he gave,
when you've worn the face you swore you'd never wear,
you stop throwing stones from the mortal grave.
As if suddenly you recognize the hunger beneath the stare
and instantly know exactly what it was he came to take.

The priest can preach it. The saint can teach it clean.
But compassion born from comfort is a pleasant lie;
some lesson mouthed but never really seen.
The devil makes you live the reason why,
makes sure you know each stop on what it means.

He is no more good than this is a sermon.
But grace that's never suffered has a hollow ring.
The ones he's touched walk different from the joint
of knowing and the weight that knowing brings...
they love like people burned, and that's the point.
