She holds what you buried when beauty grew bearable,
the want you dissolved into something more wearable,
the wound that healed into a wall,
a wall into a nation, standing tall
and you inside it unrepairable.

Name it's borders "landscape" and call it "peace"
demanding of your longing its release,
perform the auditions and lose the thread
and love the self you put to bed
the one who made the aching somehow cease.

A slow cessation like a softening of days,
a light shone faint through amber haze,
and still the morning glass remembers you,
your name misting the cold to something true
a seeing beyond this endless maze.

She calibrates and sets the clock back through regret
to where you stood so purely in your debt
to longing, where the longing was indeed
the hour the careful architect of need
dismantled what remembrace can't forget.

Forget the hand and that room full of open vow
the Tuesday beauty no one would allow
go back and find that thread, retrieve the grain
of who you were before objections abstained
practicality became your here and now.

Before that word was spoken cold and uttered clean
and something in you nodded to convene
with compromise, called the dying growth,
kept walking, disciplined, and shed the oath
of everything you were at seventeen.

At seventeen the temple was sacred fire
the north rang full of consequence in choir
she holds it all in amber, dead and true
the precise and unrelenting residue
of everything you gave to desire.

Beyond the searching and before the gate
in the breath before you whispered you were late
go down to where the first things always dwell,
carry nothing save the thing you fell
into the once you learned to wait.
