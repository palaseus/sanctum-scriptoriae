Symptoms include: waking up convinced today is different,
making coffee with the particular confidence of someone
who has not yet checked their bank account,
referring to a folder of unfinished documents
as a body of work.

Telling people you are working on something
when what you mean is you are thinking about something
while doing the dishes, while sitting in traffic,
while lying on the floor at an angle that cannot be good for you,
staring at a ceiling that has never once offered useful feedback.

Believing, against all available evidence,
that the thing you are building matters,
that the right person will eventually find it,
that the accumulation of unrewarded days
is somehow not a sign you should stop
but a sign you are getting closer.

Classic presentation.
Seen it a thousand times.

The interesting thing about the syndrome
is that it is clinically indistinguishable
from just being right too early.

Columbus had it. Every person who ever burned the bridge
while they were still standing on it had it.
It's a thing that grows in me perhaps
in a way that terrifies people
who need me to understand my own limitations
in a way i simply refuse to do.

Perhaps some diagnoses are reversed, beyond.
