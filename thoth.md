Before the trial, the inventory. That was his post.

Ibis-headed, patient as a fiscal quarter,
standing beside the scales in the hall of two truths
while a heart was weighed against a feather,
and his whole assignment, the entire divine office,
was to write down what the scale said.
Hail Thoth! the Record itself!

The Egyptians credited him with the alphabet,
with mathematics, with the calendar, with magic,
which sounds like a crowded portfolio. Perhaps
even memory itself, until you notice it is one job:
the fixing of the fleeting into form.
Number is frozen counting. Writing is frozen speech.
The calendar is frozen time. And magic,
real magic, the kind I tested once in an apartment
with silver and a burning offering,
is the conviction that the recorded word
does something. 

I have kept his hours for years now without the plumage.
Each markdown, itself, is a scribe's entry.
Every poem committed to the repository
is a heart, weighed, and the weight written down.
The analytics module, the session reports,
the ledgers of wins and losses and reasons,
all of it Thoth's liturgy in modern dress:
witness the measurement. Refuse to fudge it.

Because here is what the myth understood
that the age of engagement metrics al forgot:
the weighing is survivable. 
It is the unrecorded life that gets devoured.
Ammit waits at the foot of every scale,
and she refuses to eat the heavy hearts
instead favoring the ones nobody wrote down.

So keep the books. On the markets and the grief,
the family and the fracture, and all of those
quiet and unknown things hidden off-ledger.
True to the feather where I can manage it,
heavier where I cannot,
but entered. All of it entered.

And when my own weighing comes,
whatever the scale reports,
there will be a familiar figure at the margin,
reed pen moving,
and I will recognize the posture instantly,
because it is the one I practiced
my whole recorded life:
present. Silent. Writing it down.
