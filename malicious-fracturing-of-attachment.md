There is no such count in any code I have searched,
and I have searched the way only the aggrieved search,
past midnight, past relevance, past the point
where the reading is research
and into the region where it is ritual.

Assault has a statute. Theft has a schedule of degrees.
But sever a child from a grandmother by strategy,
poison a name slowly at holiday tables,
engineer the distance and then cite the distance
as evidence the love was never real,
and the law shrugs. No corpus, it says. Show me the body.

The body is the attachment. That is my filing.

Science has the photographs if the court wants exhibits.
Bonds are not metaphors. They are wiring,
laid down in infancy, load-bearing for life,
and there are hands that cut them with intent,
with malice aforethought, over years,
which in any honest jurisdiction
is the definition of premeditation.

I am drafting the missing statute here, in verse,
the only venue that accepts my jurisdiction.

Whoever so willfully, and with design,
fractures the attachment of one soul to another,
by isolation, by slander, by the rationing of access,
shall be guilty in the first degree
of the only theft that appreciates,
since what was stolen was time,
and time compounds,
and the victims pay the interest for generations.

Sentencing is not mine to carry out. I know the verse.
Vengeance belongs to a higher court, already seized of the matter.

My role is witness. So witnessed:
the fracture was far less weather than it was work.
Somebody did this. Their hands were warm.
And the record, this record,
will outlive every hand involved.
