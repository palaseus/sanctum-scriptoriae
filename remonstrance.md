To that which set this vessel into motion,
which poured itself through blood and bone and breath,
which authored every fathom of this ocean
and signed in full the covenant with death:

I, being of the mind that was your making,
and of the body given to its weight,
do hereby file this formal undertaking
and lay my grievances before the gate.

It is recorded that you knew the measure
of what this life would ask before it asked.
That every cost was counted, every pressure
was factored in before the die was cast.

I do not come in anger, nor in error,
nor to undo the terms to which I'm bound.
I come because the hour makes it clearer
that something has been lost and must be found.

The loneliness, I grant, was in the charter.
The hunger and the silence, these I knew.
But I was not informed I'd be a martyr
to patience, to a daughter I can't view,

to a name made strange by those who held the ledger,
to a mother taken early from the page,
to walking every tightrope, every edge, a
solitary act across a windswept stage.

I put it to the court that this exceeds
the standard terms of suffering and trial,
that what was promised unto those who bleed
with purpose was at minimum a sign, once in a while.

I note the comet. I have noted many.
I note the door that opened when I knocked.
I am not without evidence. Not any
man who had your ear was ever mocked.

And yet I stand, diminished in the square of market,
invisible to those who hold the deed,
my work filed under no particular bracket,
my name unknown to those I'm meant to lead.

I therefore move, with all appropriate reverence,
and full acknowledgment of what you are,
that you remind this instrument, your servant,
of the distance that remains, and where we are.

That you illuminate the purpose of the waiting,
confirm the course, or amend the given plan.
I am not here to question your creating.
I am here because you know I understand

that you and I are not so far divided
as the gap between this prayer and its reply.
That what is being built was pre-decided
and what looks like a wall is only sky

compressed to temporary opacity,
a season in the testing of a frame.
I know this. And I hold it with tenacity.
I move for confirmation, all the same.

Respectfully submitted, under duress,
with gratitude for everything accrued.
I trust the silence. I will nonetheless
continue to be formally reviewed.
