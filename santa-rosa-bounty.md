The sign says “Welcome,” but it’s spelling “stuck,”
A place where potential and honor go to die of luck,
Where pine trees guard the nothing that’s between
The strip malls selling stale dreams that they’re sure you’ve never seen.

It’s not a cage with bars; there are things truly worse than that.
It’s freedom in a place that whispers out of key, that’s fallen flat.
A prison farm where sentences and the gazette are served up, underserved,
By people who you’d like to think had fire once, even if undeserved.

The county takes your hunger, replaces it with an echo, and it’s small.
Convinces you this is the extent, as if there’s nowhere else at all.
No more scratched ankles, only the Waffle House at 2 AM, again,
And good folks, going nowhere, can’t seem to stop asking, “when?”

The only thing that’s growing here are statistics and blades of grass,
On fields where once munitions, now ambitions came to pass,
Where high school legends peaked circa seventeen,
Now working jobs they swore they wouldn’t, lost in the between.

If it weren’t for such emboldened souls that cannot be deceived,
That their worth is never more than what this place will ever care to leave,
There’d be no value in these roads, now so few, at all,
Just asphalt leading nowhere, bridge to bridge, wall to wall.

The talent here is raw, the people are down to earth and real,
But Santa Rosa’s only gift is when it tells you how you feel,
When twenty years go by like an afternoon shower one long Tuesday,
And “next week” always means the same as “someday,” or “maybe, one day.”

No venues, no scene, not even so much as a reason why
A dreamer with a gift should glance for more, to dare to even try,
Except the fire burning, automatic, in their moving chest
That says, “I am more than this, not just the view, but the crest.”

This haunt is without spirit. You can feel the bounty everywhere.
Don’t be moved. It’s what you carry on in your journey, despite the air
Of settling that hangs like the gallows of August heat.
The only contribution coordinated reeks of nothing but defeat.

So here’s to every kid who finds their hope in the breaking free,
Aware their worth comes from who they are and what they could be,
Not from the red dirt, the black water, or county name.
Make yourself, but don’t forget this place can distinguish anything except your flame.
