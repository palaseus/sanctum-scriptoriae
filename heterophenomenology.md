The philosopher's proposal, in fairness, was polite:
we cannot enter your experience,
so we will interview it from outside,
record your reports like a court stenographer,
and treat your inner life as testimony,
admissible, but never verified.

Science as a deposition of the soul.

I have been heterophenomenologized my whole life
by cheaper practitioners.
Caseworkers with clipboards. Relatives with theories.
The State of Florida, which took extensive notes
on my behavior while declining, categorically,
to ask what it was like in here.

They compile the file. The file becomes the man.
Anyone who has been reduced to a file
knows the specific cold of it,
your weather described by satellites
that never once got rained on.

Here is my counter-methodology.
I keep a first-person record they cannot subpoena.
These poems are the lab notes.
Every metaphor is a data point
from the one instrument with access,
calibrated by grief, checked against scripture,
peer-reviewed by God in the secret place.

Let them keep their third-person science.
It is honest about its limits, at least,
more honest than the uncle with the diagnosis.

But when the two accounts conflict,
when the file says stable and the room says otherwise,
when the transcript says he seemed fine,
I will trust the witness who was actually inside,
the one who took the stand every morning,
swore himself in,
and testified by continuing.
